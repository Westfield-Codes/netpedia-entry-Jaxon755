Do You Know Web Browsers can access our personal data by using web cookies?


Definition / Description

These are some web browsers that can access your data when ever you use them. Browsers or Websites may track your online activity by using a cookie or pixel to identify you even after you leave the site. Or they may use device fingerprinting â€” a technique that uses your browser's unique configurations and settings to track your activity.

Examples

First-party cookies are directly created by the website you are using. These are generally safer, as long as you are browsing reputable websites or ones that have not been compromised by a recent data breach or cyberattack.

Third-party cookies are generated by websites that are different from the pages that the users are currently surfing, usually because they're linked to ads on that page. Third-party cookies let advertisers or analytics companies track an individual's browsing history across the web on any sites that contain their ads.
